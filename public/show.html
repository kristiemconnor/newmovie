Actors



Bill Murray


William James Murray was born in Wilmette, Illinois, on the 21st of September in 1950. He was one of eight children.

Bill Murray began his acting career on the National Lampoon Radio Hour in 1975 with comedians John Belushi & Dan Aykroyd . From 1977 to 1980 Murray performed in Saturday Night Live , a comedy sketch show on NBC , where he popularized a seedy, shifty comedic persona. He launched his film career with a string of commercial hits, including Meatballs (1979), Caddyshack (1980), and Stripes (1981). In 1984 Murray starred with Aykroyd and Harold Ramis in Ghostbusters, which became one of the highest-grossing films of the decade.

A run of unsuccessful films led Murray into a self-imposed hiatus until he directed and starred in Quick Change (1990). After playing a burnt out weatherman in the existential comedy Groundhog Day (1993), Murray began tackling more dramatic and challenging parts, including supporting roles in Ed Wood (directed by Tim Burton, 1994) and Rushmore (directed by Wes Anderson, 1998).
